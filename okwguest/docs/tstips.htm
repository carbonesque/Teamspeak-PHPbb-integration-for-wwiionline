Voice -Comm Tips

The voice-comm software we use in the game is very similar to CB radio communications. Only one user can speak at a time. If two users try to transmit at the same time, both transmissions become unintelligible. Take care not to interrupt other transmissions as well as keep your own transmissions short and to the point.

General Transmission protocol:

Begin all of your transmissions by identifying yourself. Do not expect other players to recognize you by your voice. It wastes way too much time figuring out who said "matty on my tag". By the time we find out who you are, the matty could be gone, or killing you.

Example: FuZe here, I have 2 a13s southeast of Zand AB, 500 meters inbound, Over.

When addressing another player call their handle first, and then follow it with your own handle.

Example: “BossHogg, this is FuZe, do you copy? Over”

SPOT Reports

Contact reports or SPOT (army lingo) when you see enemy units you need to always give out a contact report. A major benefit of voice-comms like TeamSpeak is that it allows other players to know about what you see, and vice versa. The comm should not be abused, and you should keep your transmitions brief to give other players a chance to speak. Just remember to think before you transmit.

Size : Report the size of the force. How many tanks, planes, infantry? You don't have to be exact, but the more descriptive, the better.

Position : Report the position of your contact. Give a direction relative to a landmark or road as well as the approximate distance. Avoid using your tag as a landmark.

Observation : Describe what you see them doing and what direction are they heading or facing. Are they rallying? Charging? Hulling down?

Transmit : Only after you have the above info formed in your head do you transmit.

Open Comms/Closed Comms:

With a large amount of players on a channel (10+) comms discipline become important. The channel commander may choose to call for closed comms. This means that players must use breaks first and wait for the channel commander to clear the comm in order to be able to continue. During closed comms all transmissions must concern the current battlefield situation.

Normal Break:

N2deep: “Break”

FuZe: “Go Break”

N2deep: “This is n2deep. My team is in position”

Contact Break:

BossHogg: “Break for contact”

FuZe: “Go contact”

BossHogg: “BossHogg here, I have 2 a13s north east of Zand AB, 500 meters”

Closed comms are not always necessary. During long trips or in other situations where the players are not in the combat zone” a channel commander may call for open comms. Players need not use breaks in order to speak and are free to talk about almost anything.

Using these protocols, up to 40 players on a single channel may be managed easily.

Spelling on TeamSpeak:

Sometimes you may need to spell something out (like a player or town name) on teamspeak. Using these phonetics when spelling help you to be understood:

Alpha Bravo Charlie Delta Echo Foxtrot Golf Hotel India
Juliet Kilo Lima Mike November Oscar Papa Quincy Romeo
Sierra Tango Uniform Victor Whiskey Xray Yankee Zulu
